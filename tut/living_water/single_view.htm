"Jesus According to Mark "

Pastor Laura Guy

March 5 , 2006

 

Here's an instant replay of what happened in worship:

  • We heard about a group of seminary students who were surprised to discover that when they heard the entire Gospel of Mark recited out loud, the actions and questions of the disciples often seemed comical. Why would Mark portray these pillars of the faith as naive and even foolish at times?
  • We learned that Mark was the first Gospel written; Matthew and Luke used Mark when they were composing their own Gospel accounts. Mark - thought to be John Mark, Peter's interpreter - wrote down the important information about the life, death and resurrection of Jesus as the disciples and other eye-witnesses were dying out. His Gospel is fast-paced, beginning abruptly and ending abruptly (if you consider 16:8 the ending of the original Gospel).
  • We noticed how Mark lets the audience in on a "secret" in the very first verse - Jesus is the Son of God. Throughout the book, others proclaim Jesus as the Son of God - God does, demons do, Jesus himself does, and even a Roman centurion realizes it! But the disciples never do figure it out.
  • We looked at Mark 10:35-45 where James and John ask Jesus if they can sit by him in heaven. This is after they have spent all kinds of time with Jesus, hearing him teach and seeing him perform miracles. They still think his ministry is all about getting glory, and they want a piece of it for themselves. Jesus once more patiently tries to explain that his kingdom is not about being great so others can serve you. It's about lowering yourself and serving others to be the greatest. The cup Jesus drank is the cup of love to the point of death and beyond. James and John had no idea what they were even asking!
  • We realized that Mark is telling us that, like the original disciples, we can also spend a lot of time with Jesus and still not understand what he is all about. If our prayers to him are focused on what he can do for us, how he can bless us, how we want to be in a mansion in heaven, then we have missed the point along with James and John. But, like them, when we know Jesus as the crucified Savior and risen Lord, then we can begin to understand the meaning of who Jesus was.
  • We prayed that God would forgive our foolishness, even as Jesus forgave his disciples when they didn't understand. We asked to meet Jesus as the crucified and risen one so that we could begin to understand.
  • We celebrated communion at the welcome table. Jesus invites us to come with whatever is on our hearts - joy, emptiness or brokenness - and he meets us there.
